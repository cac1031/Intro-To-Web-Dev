Planet Facts

Mercury
Despite being closest to the Sun, Mercury has ice on its surface. These areas are permanently shaded from the Sun's intense heat.
Venus
If you stood on Venus' surface, you would see the Sun rise in the west and set in the east.
Earth
Earth is the only planet not named after a mythological god. The name "Earth" is derived from Old English and Germanic words meaning "ground" or "soil."
Mars
Mars has the tallest volcano in the solar system. Olympus Mons is about 22km high, nearly three times the height of Mount Everest.
Jupiter
Jupiter doesn't technically orbit the Sun. Instead, the Sun and Jupiter both orbit around a common center of mass, located just above the Sun's surface.
Saturn
Saturn is less dense than water. If there were a bathtub big enough, Saturn would float!
Uranus
Uranus rotates on its side, rolling around the Sun almost like a ball. This unique tilt likely resulted from a massive collision early in its history.
Neptune
Neptune's striking blue color is due to the presence of methane in its atmosphere, which absorbs red light and reflects blue.
Pluto
Pluto was reclassified as a dwarf planet by the International Astronomical Union (IAU) in 2006.
